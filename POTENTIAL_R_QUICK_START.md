# Potential R - Quick Start Guide

## What Is It?

A system to track whether your profit targets are **too conservative**. By recording how far price actually ran past your target, we can analyze patterns and suggest better targets that could increase your average win size.

## The Concept

You're targeting **0.75R** on most trades, but they actually run to **1.0R** or more on average?

That means you're **leaving money on the table**. The analytics will tell you:
- How much further price typically runs
- What target would capture more
- How much more profit you'd make per trade

## How to Use (3 Simple Steps)

### Step 1: Log "Potential R" on Winning Trades

After a trade wins and closes:

1. Go to **Trades** page
2. Find the trade in the list
3. Click the **â‹® (three dots)** menu on the right
4. Select **"Add Potential R"** (only shows for wins)
5. Enter the **actual R value** price reached
   - Example: If your target was 0.75R but price ran to 1.2R, enter `1.2`
6. Click **Save**

That's it! One click per trade.

### Step 2: Build Up Data

Log potential R for several trades (aim for 5-10 per target size):
- When you target 0.75R, log what it actually reached
- When you target 1.0R, log what it actually reached
- Patterns emerge as you collect more data

### Step 3: Check the Analytics

1. Go to **Analytics** dashboard
2. Scroll down to find **"Potential R Analysis"** card
3. See the AI insight at the top
4. Review your target performance
5. Check the scenario modeling

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be honest about the numbers**
   - Enter what price actually reached, not what you hoped
   - Use the high (for longs) or low (for shorts)

2. **Log consistently**
   - Even if you forget sometimes, that's OK
   - The more trades you log, the better the analysis

3. **Check monthly**
   - Patterns might change seasonally
   - Market conditions evolve
   - Adjust accordingly

4. **Don't change everything at once**
   - Test the suggestion on a few trades first
   - See if your win rate stays the same
   - Then decide to commit

5. **Consider the context**
   - If one day was an outlier (very volatile), maybe skip it
   - If market conditions changed, data might be outdated
   - Use it as a guide, not gospel

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to log it for every trade?**
A: No, it's optional. But the more you log, the better the analysis. Aim for at least 5 per target size.

**Q: What if I already have trades without potential R?**
A: No problem! You can edit them anytime by clicking the menu again.

**Q: What if my targets vary wildly?**
A: The analytics groups by target size, so each size gets its own analysis. Use what makes sense for you.

**Q: Should I immediately change my targets?**
A: Not necessarily. Wait for 10+ trades at each target before committing. One lucky day isn't a pattern.

**Q: Does this guarantee better results?**
A: No. It shows what *could* happen based on historical patterns. Future markets might behave differently. Use it as guidance.
